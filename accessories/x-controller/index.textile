<p>Welcome to the X-Controller assembly instructions!  The images shown at the beginning of each step show the finished results of that step.  This is to provide context for what you're about to start working on, and offer those eager and willing to figure things out on their own a way to skim through more quickly.  Make sure to click on the thumbnails to see more details.</p>

|\3{color:#fff;background: #383838}. **Tools** |
|\3. Phillips Screwdriver |
|\3. 2.5mm Allen Wrench |
|\3. 3mm Allen Wrench |
|\3. 5.5mm Open End Wrench (Adjustable Wrench or Pliers) |

<p>Bear in mind that these instructions were completed with a pre-production X-Controller, so you may find a few inconsistencies between what is pictured and what's in front of you.</p>

  <img src="P8200931Editcopy.jpg">

  <h3>1. Attach Feet (OPTIONAL)</h3>
  <img src="P9231171Editcopy.jpg">
  <p>Attach the four rubber "feet" to the bottom of the chassis.  Flip the chassis over and twist the foot through the hole.  Once the foot is partially in, try using your fingernail to bring it the rest of the way through.  This is an option step and the holes can be used to mount the chassis to a work table.</p>

  <h3>4. Attach Power Supply to Chassis</h3>
  <img src="P8200936Editcopy.jpg">
  <p>Using the M4 button head cap screws, attach the power supply through the mounting holes on the underside of the chassis.  The threaded holes on the base of the power supply are delicate, so try not to overtighten the screws when assembling.
  </p>
  <div class="note">
  	<i class="fa fa-hand-o-right"></i>
  	<span class="note-text">
    	*Note: The chassis is not symmetrical.*  The interface PCB side of the enclosed power supply should be on the same end of the chassis with the mounting holes set furthest away.  The power supply interface PCB will be flush with the end of the chassis when installed correctly.
  	</span>
  </div>

  <h3>2. Attach Stops</h3>
  <img src="P8200944Editcopy.jpg">
  <p>Install the M4 button head cap screws in the threaded holes on the side of the chassis.  These screws locate the back of the controller board and make the face of the board flush with the chassis.</p>

  <h3>3. Wire Controller Board</h3>
  <img src="P8200993Editcopy.jpg">
  <p>Attach the cables to the power supply interface PCB.  The cables can only be connected in one orientation, so if they don't attach well double check them and try again.  Try to orient them so that the wires can easily travel from board to board.  Make sure to tuck the e-stop cable along the edge of the enclosed power supply to prevent any sort of snagging or pinching.</p>

  <h3>1. Dissasemble E-Stop Button</h3>
  <img src="P8201008Editcopy.jpg">
  <p>Remove the button from the screw terminals by gently pulling on the yellow tab. With the button upside down (as pictured) pull the tab towards you and then to the right to disengage the two parts.  Unscrew the nut attached to the base of the button. This nut will be used to secure the button to the enclosure.</p>

Slide the top panel into the channels of the chassis, far enough back so you can access the Mini USB port on the main controller board.</p>

  <h3>4. Wire E-Stop And Attach Front Panel</h3>
  <img src="P8201081Editcopy.jpg">
  <p>Loosen the phillips head screws on the screw terminals and attach the ends of the e-stop cable to each side of the NC screw terminal as pictured. Tighten the screws on the screw terminals to hold the cable in place.  To attach the wired screw terminal to the button, *line up the "TOP" marking on the button with the "TOP" marking on the screw terminal.*  Once in postion, move the yellow tab towards the back of the assembly to lock in place.  Attach the front panel using the included M4 socket head cap screws.</p>
